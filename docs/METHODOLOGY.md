# Methodology

## Research Approach

This section outlines the research approach that was taken.

1. **Identify Objectives**: Define the research questions and objectives clearly.
2. **Literature Review**: Conduct a comprehensive review of existing literature to establish the context for the research.
3. **Hypothesis Development**: Develop hypotheses that the research aims to test.

## Evaluation Metrics

To assess the performance of the proposed solutions, the following evaluation metrics were utilized:

- **Accuracy**: Measure the proportion of correct predictions in the dataset.
- **Precision**: Calculate the ratio of true positive predictions to the total number of positive predictions.
- **Recall**: Measure the ability to find all relevant instances in the dataset.
- **F1 Score**: The harmonic mean of precision and recall, providing a balance between the two metrics.

## Benchmarking Procedures

The following benchmarking procedures were implemented to compare the performance:

1. **Dataset Preparation**: Ensure that the datasets used are standard benchmarks in the field.
2. **Cross-Validation**: Implement k-fold cross-validation to ensure the reliability of results.
3. **Comparison with Baselines**: Evaluate against established baseline models to highlight improvements or shortcomings.

## Reproducibility Protocols

To ensure that the results are reproducible, the following protocols were established:

- **Version Control**: All code and data were stored using a version control system (e.g., Git).
- **Documentation**: Comprehensive documentation accompanying all experimental setups and configurations.
- **Data Availability**: Make datasets publicly available for verification and further research.
